James E. Mack ~ Guest Conductor, Soloist

James E. Mack: graduated from Northwestern University, where he majored in clarinet performance as a student of Domenico Decaprio. After service as principal clarinetist and soloist with a military band, he did graduate study at UCLA and California State University, Los Angeles and further clarinet study with Vito Susca. From 1964 to 1998, Professor Mack served as Director of Instrumental Music at EI Camino College, where he conducted the Symphony Orchestra, Community/Symphonic Band, Jazz Band, small ensembles, and was music director for 21 Spring Musicals. Mack was a member of the Young Musicians Foundation (Debut) Orchestra under Andre Previn, as well as principal clarinetist with a number of regional orchestras. While on sabbatical he received additional clarinet and chamber music instruction from Canadian clarinetist Robert Sheffield of the Johannesen International School of the Arts in Victoria, British Columbia. He has also served as soloist and guest conductor with numerous amateur and professional groups and is currently principal clarinetist with the Carson-Dominguez Hills Symphony Orchestra. While still a part-time faculty member at E1 Camino, where he teaches clarinet and Clarinet Choir, he continues to perform regularly with a variety of ensembles in the Greater Los Angeles area.
