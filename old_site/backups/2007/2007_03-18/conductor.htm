Richard Babcock

Richard Babcock's first close contact with music was the study of the trumpet at age ten. He advanced to composing in junior high school, winning several local competitions over the next ten years. Conducting his own works and learning other instruments were his next advances. Richard studied music at the University of Utah, specializing in woodwinds. After graduation in 1980, he spent the next three years teaching music and working as a freelance musician. In 1983, he was accepted into the Master of Fine Arts program in composition at the California Institute of the Arts.

In 1987, he was appointed Director of Orchestras for Chadwick School in Palos Verdes, where he currently serves. The experiences as conductor, composer and teacher have developed in Richard an eagerness to push farther into new regions, enjoying the challenge of taking musical risks.
