Joseph A. Valenti

Joseph A. Valenti is well known to Southern California music audiences. A resident of the Palos Verdes Peninsula and graduate of the Westlake College of Music, he has been the Music Director and Conductor of the Peninsula Symphony for 30 years. He studied conducting with Dr. Alfred Sendrey, Pierre Monteux, and Serge Koussevitzky, and has led many orchestras and choral groups including the San Francisco Symphony, the Warner Brothers Orchestra, the Hollywood Opera Workshop, the Senior Symphony of Los Angeles, the North American Male Chorus, and Los Cancioneros. Prominent artists who have performed under his baton include pianists Leonard Pennario and Renee and Mona Golabek, violinists Alice Schoenfeld and Sidney Harth, cellists Leonard Rose and Eleanore Schoenfeld, guitarists Laurindo Almeida and The Romeros, trumpeter Raphael Mendez, and many others.

Maestro Valenti is himself an accomplished trumpet player, performing with his own band and other society orchestra and jazz groups. He has also performed with Frank Sinatra, Bing Crosby, Steve Allen, Richard Tucker, Eartha Kitt and many others, and has recorded with David Rose and the Art Linkletter show.

In addition to his busy life as a performer, Maestro Valenti is a composer, arranger and teacher. He has arranged and orchestrated music for the Church of Religious Science for 26 years. He has composed three symphonies and has a fourth in progress. He is the author of a number of books for music study and practice, was the Music Director and a teacher at the Pedrini School of Music, teaches privately in his own studio and school, and has been a Staff Professor at Harbor College for the last 22 years.
